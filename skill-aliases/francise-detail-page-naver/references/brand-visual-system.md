# Brand Visual System

Use this reference for `$francise-detail-page-naver` whenever a named franchise brand appears. The goal is to avoid generic AI-looking cards and make the final images feel like edited real-photo brand content.

## Core Rule

Every brand must receive its own visual system. Do not reuse the same brown template across brands.

Use real photos as the main visual material. The design should feel edited from field photos, not generated from a blank template.

## Quality Direction

Prefer a calm, premium, practical look:

- Large real photos.
- Minimal Korean copy inside the image.
- Generous margin and clear alignment.
- Brand accent color used with restraint.
- No childish sticker effects.
- No generic icon clutter.
- No heavy rounded boxes unless they help the information.
- No dense bullet lists inside images.
- Use separate blog text for explanations.

When moving away from the user's outsourced examples, aim for the clarity a senior product designer at Apple or Toss might prefer: simple hierarchy, strong spacing, precise typography, and confident restraint.

## Photo Usage Rules

- At least 8 of 12 final cards should make a real store photo the main visual.
- Avoid using photos only as dark blurred backgrounds.
- Crop photos intentionally:
  - signage: brand recognition and visibility
  - exterior: access, road, entrance, first impression
  - interior: cleanliness, BI, seating, operation flow
  - counter/menu: turnover, order flow, management complexity
- If a photo is strong, let it breathe. Do not cover it with large panels.
- If a photo is weak, use it smaller inside a clean editorial layout.

## Layout Families

Use a mix of these, not one repeated card:

1. Full photo hero: large photo, small brand mark, short title.
2. Editorial photo card: photo 65-80%, text 20-35%.
3. Clean condition summary: warm/white background, metrics with thin lines.
4. Photo evidence card: one photo plus two or three short check labels.
5. Final CTA card: calm conclusion and disclaimer, no loud advertising style.

## Design QA

Reject the draft if any of these are true:

- It looks like a generic AI card template.
- It could be used for any brand by only changing the logo color.
- The real photo feels secondary.
- Text boxes dominate the image.
- Typography feels like a school presentation.
- The brand palette is missing.
- The same layout repeats more than three times in a row.
- Korean is broken, too small, or visually crowded.
