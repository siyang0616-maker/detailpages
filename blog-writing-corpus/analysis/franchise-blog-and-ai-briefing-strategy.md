## What To Keep From The Existing Posts

- Start with a direct problem or decision question, not a long greeting.
- Use region + brand + startup/takeover intent early in the title or opening.
- Keep the writing practical: cost, location, buyer anxiety, operational burden, and risk.
- Frame takeover as a way to reduce uncertainty through existing-store verification.
- Use repeated buyer concerns:
  - "new startup is risky"
  - "brand alone is not enough"
  - "location and sustainable sales must be checked"
  - "verified existing store can be more rational than blind new opening"
- Use consultant viewpoint, but avoid sounding like an official headquarters notice.

## What To Improve

- Add clear subheadings every 3-5 paragraphs.
- Convert long persuasive paragraphs into answerable sections.
- Make each section answer one search intent:
  - Is this brand still worth considering?
  - Why compare takeover with new startup?
  - What should be checked before contract?
  - What does the startup cost include?
  - What risks remain?
- Put the conclusion or recommendation near the top, then explain the reason.
- Separate provided facts from assumptions.
- Replace vague confidence with concrete checking logic.
- Add "field-check" language: photos, store condition, sales data, lease/contract, nearby competition, equipment, included scope.

## What To Discard

- Generic greetings such as "안녕하세요" or long introductions.
- Empty praise such as "최고의 선택", "안전 자산", "성공 확정", "무조건 추천".
- Overused brand bragging without store-level evidence.
- Repeated sentences that restate "좋은 브랜드입니다" without adding a new check point.
- Definite performance claims unless backed by provided data.
- Claims like "매출 안정성 보장", "실패 확률을 낮춘다" when evidence is not shown.
- Review-like exaggeration that feels like an ad rather than a consultant note.

## Preferred Conversion Rules

Use this style conversion when drafting:

- "추천입니다" -> "검토해볼 만한 조건입니다"
- "안전 자산입니다" -> "초기 리스크를 비교적 구체적으로 확인할 수 있는 유형입니다"
- "성공 가능성이 높습니다" -> "입지, 매출 자료, 운영 조건을 함께 보면 판단 근거가 생깁니다"
- "매출이 보장됩니다" -> "기존 매출 자료를 기준으로 흐름을 확인할 수 있습니다"
- "브랜드 파워가 강합니다" -> "인지도는 장점이지만, 실제 판단은 상권과 점포 조건을 같이 봐야 합니다"
- "월매출이 높습니다" -> "월매출 자료가 제공된 경우, 비용 대비 적정성을 따져볼 수 있습니다"
- "초보도 쉽습니다" -> "운영 매뉴얼이 있는 브랜드라도 인력, 피크타임, 재료 관리 부담은 확인해야 합니다"

## Naver Search And AI-Briefing Direction

Naver Search Advisor states that search optimization is about organizing content so the search engine can clearly understand what users want from the page. Naver also says it improves systems around spam, user feedback, and ranking signals that reflect how users judge good documents.

For franchise blog/detail-page content, this means:

- Write for a specific question, not just a keyword.
- Use descriptive subheadings that can stand alone.
- Include concise answer blocks near the top of important sections.
- Avoid duplicate-looking posts by changing the angle per region, brand, and buyer concern.
- Use real photos and specific store-check language to show first-hand context.
- Make claims traceable to provided facts or mark them as "확인 필요".
- Include caution sections; balanced writing is more trustworthy than pure sales copy.

## Recommended Blog Structure

1. Title
   - Include region + brand + 창업/양도양수 + real question.
   - Example pattern: `수원시 메가커피 창업 양도양수, 신규창업보다 먼저 비교해야 하는 이유`

2. Opening answer
   - 2-4 short paragraphs.
   - No greeting.
   - State the conclusion carefully: "무조건 좋다" is not allowed.

3. Why this question matters
   - Explain the buyer's real anxiety: cost, location, competition, sales uncertainty.

4. Brand-level context
   - Explain brand recognition, customer demand, operation style.
   - Do not let this replace store-level verification.

5. Store-level checks
   - Location, visibility, floor, size, seating, counter, equipment, nearby competition, sales records.

6. New startup vs takeover
   - Compare uncertainty, time cost, additional setup cost, and what can be checked in advance.

7. Cost review
   - Use "창업비용" and provided facts.
   - Do not expose sensitive deposit/rent fields unless explicitly requested.

8. Checklist before decision
   - Sales data, headquarters transfer approval, lease/contract, equipment condition, included scope, staffing.

9. Practical conclusion
   - Summarize who should consider it and who should be cautious.

10. Consultation CTA
   - Offer analysis, not guaranteed success.

## Detail Page Translation

When turning blog tone into a 12-cut detail page:

- Cut 1: Answer the buyer's real question immediately.
- Cut 2: Show the risk of choosing by brand name only.
- Cut 3: Explain why takeover deserves comparison.
- Cut 4: Show provided facts and mark unknowns.
- Cut 5: Use exterior/signage photos for visibility and access.
- Cut 6: Use interior/counter photos for operation flow.
- Cut 7: Use equipment/photos for included-scope review.
- Cut 8: Use menu/product photos for demand and operation complexity.
- Cut 9: Compare new startup vs takeover.
- Cut 10: Review startup cost logic.
- Cut 11: Show pre-contract checklist.
- Cut 12: CTA for consulting review.

## QA Checklist

- Does the content answer a real buyer question in the first 5 lines?
- Are there descriptive subheadings?
- Is every strong claim tied to provided facts?
- Are assumptions marked as "확인 필요"?
- Is the writing practical rather than promotional?
- Does it avoid long greeting/opening filler?
- Does it include risks and checks, not just advantages?
- Does it use real photos or field-check context where available?
- Does it avoid duplicated structure across brands and regions?
